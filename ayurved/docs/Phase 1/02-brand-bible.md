## Brand Name Options (For Founder Decision)

| Option | Rationale | Risk |
|--------|-----------|------|
| **Charaka** | Direct, authoritative, owns the source | Sanskrit — needs education |
| **Charaka Co.** | Modern, approachable | Generic suffix |
| **Charaka Living** | Lifestyle framing | "Living" is overused |
| **The Charaka Method** | Positions as a system | Long name |
| **Arogya by Charaka** | Arogya = health/wellbeing | Two unfamiliar words |

**Recommendation:** **CHARAKA** — one word, no suffix. The education effort to explain it is the brand's first story. Lean into unfamiliarity — it signals depth.

## Brand Positioning

**Category:** Modern Ayurvedic philosophy + lifestyle  
**Position:** The authoritative, accessible, modern translator of the Charaka Saṁhitā  
**Against:** Commodity wellness brands that sell ingredients without philosophy  
**For:** Curious, intelligent people who want a system for living, not a supplement

### Positioning Statement
*For the intelligent health-seeker who is done with wellness trends — Charaka is the modern translation of the world's most complete ancient system for human health. Unlike supplement brands or wellness apps, Charaka offers a coherent philosophy with products, education, and tools that work together.*

## Tone of Voice Guide

### Do say:
- "According to Charaka, your digestion sets the pace for everything else."
- "This isn't a detox. It's how you'd eat during Sharad Ritu — autumn, in Charaka's calendar."
- "Your Vata is elevated. Here's what that actually means for your sleep."

### Never say:
- "This product cures..." / "Clinically proven to treat..."
- "Ancient wisdom for modern healing"  (too generic)
- "Detoxify your body" (medical-adjacent)
- "Boost your immune system" (regulatory risk)
- "Holistic healing" (overused, meaningless)

### The guiding test:
*"Would a knowledgeable Charaka scholar be proud of this sentence?"*  
If yes — publish. If no — rewrite.

## Visual Identity Principles

### Color Philosophy
The Charaka palette should feel like the world the text was written in — ancient manuscripts, raw botanicals, terracotta, forest mornings. Not clinical white, not Instagram-pink.

| Role | Description | Reference |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| **Primary** | Deep forest green | Dravya (plant matter) |
| **Secondary** | Warm terracotta / ochre | Earth, Prithvi element |
| **Accent** | Aged gold | Suvarna (gold), rasayana |
| **Neutrals** | Warm cream, parchment | Manuscript pages |
| **Dark** | Deep charcoal (not black) | Ink, depth |

**What to avoid:** Turquoise-and-white "spa blue." Hot pinks. Neon greens. Clean clinical whites. These belong to competitors, not to us.

### Typography Principles
- **Headlines:** A classical serif with character — signals authority and age
- **Body:** Clean humanist sans — modern readability
- **Accent / Sanskrit terms:** Devanagari or a respectful transliteration, sparingly
- **No:** Trendy display fonts. Script fonts. Fonts that look "Instagrammy."

### Photography Direction
- **People:** Real people in real homes, morning light, quiet rituals. Not yoga-studio photoshoots.
- **Products:** On natural surfaces — stone, wood, linen. Never on white seamless.
- **Ingredients:** In their raw, imperfect, whole form — not processed glamour shots.
- **Lifestyle:** A morning cup. A page of notes. An oil bottle by a window. Intimate, not aspirational.

## Brand Story (The Origin Narrative)

*Charaka was a physician in ancient India who, over 2,000 years ago, wrote the most complete guide to human health ever compiled. Not just medicine — a complete operating system for living: how to wake up, what to eat, how to think, how to adapt with the seasons, how to stay well before you get sick.*

*For most of history, this knowledge stayed inside Sanskrit texts and practitioners' memories. We're changing that.*

*Charaka [the brand] is the modern translation — made practical, personalized, and actually usable.*

*We don't sell supplements. We teach a system. The products are just where the system meets your daily life.*

## Brand Guardrails (Non-Negotiables)

1. **Always cite the source.** Every claim traces to Charaka Saṁhitā. We are translators, not inventors.
2. **Education before commerce.** No product goes live without educational content explaining its philosophical basis.
3. **No medical claims. Ever.** Wellness language only. All copy reviewed against this before publish.
4. **Practitioner advisory.** At least one credentialed Ayurvedic vaidya on advisory board; credited publicly.
5. **Ingredient integrity.** GMP-certified only. No synthetic additives. Traceable sourcing documented.
